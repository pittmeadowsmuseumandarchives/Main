Exhibits

MUSEUM EXHIBITS

The General Store Site of the museum hosts permanent exhibits relating to pioneer history of Pitt Meadows, which includes textiles, home arts, agricultural machinery, and more. Pitt Meadows Museum & Archives also contains contemporary exhibits room with new exhibits rotated through on the annual basis, as well as hands-on "Pioneer Gadgets"

Two exhibits to note are:
"Fresh from the Block: Inside a Pitt Meadows Dairy Barn"
"Buildings through time: Harris Road then & now"

ONLINE EXHIBIT: HARRIS ROAD - THEN & NOW

"This exhibit is based on the Pitt Meadows Museum's Harris Road indoor walking tour and is intended to introduce the viewer to a variety of buildings and sites, some that predate the incorporation of Pitt Meadows and others that were constructed early in the municipality's existence."

DISCOVERY BOXES

Each box contains objects, activities and related stories for groups and individuals to draw upon. Two fo the Four discovery boxes are available for use at or to borrow from the Pitt Meadows Museum. Included in each box is a teacher's guide to assist with using the box to it's maximum potential. This includes:
  • Prattle about Cattle
  • Hailing from Holland
  • Magic Made Visible
  • Magnificent Magnets

All three discovery boxes were developed over Summer 2011 by a student working under the Government of Canada's "Young Canada Works in Heritage Institutions" programs. In 2012, three more boxes are being developed uner the YCW program for the use at the Museum's Hoffmann & Son site. They include a box on luminscence and incandescence, a box on steam, strealing and gasoline power, and a box on magnetos and dynamos. Visit the Museum to find out more and keep checking in with us to see how our hands on demo table is progressing at the Hoffmann & Son site.
