Join: The Pitt Meadows Heritage & Museum Society

The Pitt Meadows Heritage and Museum Society is a non-profit, registered charity responsible for collecting, preserving and interpreting artifacts and archival material relating to Pitt Meadows and surrounding area.

The Society began in 1977 and incorporated in 1980. It is the governing authority of the Pitt Meadows Museum and Archives, which includes both the Old General Store (1886 c.) site andHoffmann & Son Machine Shop (1936) site, both located on Harris Road, South of the C.P.R. tracks. The Museum and Society operate under the annual fee-for service agreement with the Maple Ridge/Pitt Meadows Parks and Leisure Services Commission. The Museum’s second site, the Hoffmann and Son Machine Shop building was donated to the Society and the community of Pitt Meadows in October 1999, by siblings Hans (d.1999) and Elfriede (d.2009) Hoffmann.

“A meeting to organize a Heritage and Museum Society in Pitt Meadows was held at the Municipal Office on October 24, 1977. A constitution was later drawn up and the Pitt Meadows Heritage and Museum Society was registered under the Societies Act.”

The Society meets regularly on the second Wednesday of every month at 7:00pm in September, April, May , June and at 11:00 am from October through March. Meetings take place at the General Store site at 12294 Harris Road with parking at the rear of the building off the West Coast Express lot.

Membership dues are $10.00 annually and new members interested in preserving the heritage of their community are very welcome. For more information about joining and volunteering for the Museum Society, please contact us at 604-465-4322.
